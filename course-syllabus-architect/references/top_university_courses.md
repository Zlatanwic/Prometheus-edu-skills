## Quick research recipe per topic

1. Search `<institution> <course number> syllabus 2025` or `2026` (current year) — most syllabi live on `.edu` pages with predictable URLs.
2. If you don't find it, try `<institution> <course number> schedule` or `lectures` — schedule pages often have the lecture-by-lecture breakdown even when the syllabus is light.
3. For graduate seminars, check the professor's personal page — many top professors host their seminars on a personal site rather than the department site.
4. For the *very* current frontier (papers from the last 6 months), top university syllabi often haven't caught up. Cross-reference with NeurIPS/ICML/OSDI accepted-papers lists from the most recent edition.

## When the topic isn't in this list

This list is non-exhaustive. For unfamiliar or interdisciplinary topics (quantum computing, computational biology, robotics), search:
- `top universities <topic> course`
- `<topic> graduate course syllabus site:.edu`
- Recent ACM curriculum recommendations for the area

And lean more heavily on Tier 2 (papers) since the courseware may be sparse.
